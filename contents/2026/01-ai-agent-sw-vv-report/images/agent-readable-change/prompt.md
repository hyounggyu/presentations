# Agent Readable Change

## Purpose

Show that people already had SoT and Context before AI Agent adoption, but the key change is converting those materials into an agent-readable structure.

## Prompt

Create a 16:9 hand-drawn whiteboard style presentation illustration. The image should compare "Before" and "After" in two panels.

Left panel label: "Before". Show a human author surrounded by Excel-like tables, printed guide books, PDF stacks, meeting notes, review files, and sticky notes. Draw faint messy connection lines going through the person's head or notebook. Include short labels: "Excel SoT", "guides", "meeting notes", "review files", "working notes". The mood should be busy but not chaotic.

Right panel label: "After". Show the same materials organized into an agent-readable structure. Draw two clear boxes labeled "SoT YAML" and "Context Registry", feeding into a simple "AI Agent" node. Include small metadata tags near the registry: "source_path", "authority", "related_sot". Show the human still present as final reviewer, not replaced.

Main message: AI does not invent the structure; it reads structured versions of materials people already had. Use a clean comparison layout, black marker lines, muted blue for SoT, muted green for Context, and neutral gray for human review.

## Negative Prompt

Do not show AI replacing the person, automatic approval, sci-fi UI, glowing holograms, dense tiny text, or a fully polished stock infographic. Do not include "Final Document" or "Approved".
