# Why Existing Approach Is Hard

## Purpose

Show why the traditional regulatory documentation workflow is painful for document authors: repeated facts are scattered across many documents, updates must be manually propagated, evidence is hard to trace, and review comments arrive from multiple places.

## Slide Usage

Used in: `../index.md`, slide `왜 기존 방식이 힘든가`

## Prompt

A hand-drawn sketch style 16:9 presentation illustration on a whiteboard or notebook paper background. Show a stressed regulatory documentation author at the center, surrounded by many disconnected artifacts: Word-like documents, PDF reports, spreadsheets, meeting notes, review comments, test logs, regulatory guidance, and traceability matrices. Use simple black pen lines with a few muted accent colors such as red for pain points and blue for document links.

The sketch should clearly communicate the pain of the existing workflow:

- the same requirement copied into many different documents
- a single change causing many manual updates
- arrows crossing between documents in confusing directions
- review comments arriving from several channels
- uncertainty about which source is the latest truth
- missing or broken traceability links
- the author having to reconcile everything manually

Use visual metaphors such as duplicated sticky notes, tangled arrows, warning marks, small clocks, crossed-out links, and scattered checklists. The image should feel human, rough, and relatable, like a workshop sketch explaining a real workflow problem. Keep any text inside the image minimal and abstract; short handwritten labels are acceptable only if they are simple and generic, such as "Req", "Test", "Risk", "Review", "Latest?".

## Negative Prompt

Avoid polished corporate stock illustration, photorealism, sci-fi interfaces, glowing holograms, brand logos, real product names, readable long paragraphs, cartoon comedy, exaggerated facial expressions, and overly dense tiny text. Do not make the image look like a clean architecture diagram; it should feel like a hand-drawn problem sketch.

## Style Constraints

- Hand-drawn whiteboard or notebook sketch style
- Human and slightly messy, but still readable
- Professional regulatory documentation context
- Minimal embedded text
- Strong visual emphasis on repeated manual reconciliation
- 16:9 aspect ratio
